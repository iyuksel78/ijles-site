Aims & Scope

Research Areas Welcomed by IJLES

IJLES fosters critical and inclusive scholarship on language, teaching, learning, culture, policy, identity, technology, and social life.

Aims

The International Journal of Language, Education and Society (IJLES) encourages contributions that investigate the role of language in education and society, advance research on teaching and learning, and provide comparative or interdisciplinary insights into educational policy and practice.

Scope

Language Education

Language teaching, language learning, applied linguistics, acquisition, assessment, and pedagogy.

Educational Studies

Curriculum design, teacher education, instructional strategies, educational policy, and learning environments.

Society and Equity

Sociolinguistics, multilingualism, cultural interaction, identity, inclusion, social justice, and sustainability.

Interdisciplinary Research

Technology, policy, communication, social transformation, and practice-based studies connecting education and social life.

Article Types

IJLES welcomes original research articles, review papers, book reviews, theoretical essays, and practice-based studies. Final article type requirements and word limits will be displayed when available.
