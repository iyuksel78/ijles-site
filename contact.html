Submission Checklist

Before submitting to IJLES, authors should confirm that the manuscript is complete, anonymized for review, and prepared according to the journal's academic and ethical standards.

  • The manuscript is original and is not under review by another journal.
  • The topic fits the aims and scope of IJLES.
  • The manuscript follows APA 7th edition style.
  • The review file is anonymized for double-blind peer review.
  • All tables, figures, references, and appendices are complete.
  • Ethics approval, informed consent, and conflict of interest information are included where applicable.

Required Files

Blinded Manuscript

Main manuscript without author names, affiliations, acknowledgements, or identifying metadata.

Title Page

Article title, author names, affiliations, ORCID IDs, corresponding author email, and declarations.

Ethics Statement

Ethics approval, consent information, data availability, conflict of interest, funding, and AI disclosure.

Supplementary Files

Tables, figures, appendices, instruments, datasets, or similarity report if available.

Email Submission

Please send all submission files as email attachments. Use a clear subject line so the editorial office can identify the submission quickly.

Review Timeline

  1. Initial editorial screening: scope, formatting, completeness, and ethics check.
  2. Peer review assignment: suitable manuscripts are sent to external reviewers.
  3. Double-blind review: reviewers evaluate originality, validity, significance, and presentation.
  4. Editorial decision: accept, minor revision, major revision, revise and resubmit, or reject.
  5. Production: accepted manuscripts are prepared for publication after final checks.

Ethics Note

Authors are responsible for ensuring that their manuscript complies with IJLES research and publication ethics policies. Manuscripts involving human participants must include appropriate ethics approval and informed consent information. Plagiarism, duplicate submission, data fabrication, undisclosed conflicts of interest, and misleading authorship are not acceptable.

AI tools may be used only to support language clarity or technical preparation. Any use of AI must be disclosed, and authors remain fully responsible for the content, data, citations, and interpretation of the manuscript.
