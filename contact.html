Submission Checklist

  • The manuscript is within the journal's scope.
  • The manuscript is written in English.
  • APA 7 has been followed.
  • The work is original and is not under review elsewhere.
  • All authors approved the submission.
  • Author information is provided on a separate title page.
  • The manuscript is prepared for anonymous review.
  • Ethics approval, informed consent, funding, conflicts of interest, AI use, and data availability are reported where applicable.
  • Tables and figures are cited in the text, and references are complete and accurate.
  • All required files are uploaded or prepared as a single submission package.
  • The online submission form is completed and all required declarations are confirmed.

Required Files

Blinded Manuscript

Main manuscript without author names, affiliations, acknowledgements, or identifying metadata.

Title Page

Ethics approval, consent information, data availability, conflict of interest, funding, and AI disclosure where applicable.

Supplementary Files

Tables, figures, appendices, instruments, datasets, or similarity report if available.

Review Timeline

  1. Submission acknowledgement: acknowledgement confirms receipt only; it does not indicate acceptance.
  2. Initial editorial screening: scope, formatting, completeness, and ethics check.
  3. Peer review assignment: suitable manuscripts are sent to three independent external reviewers.
  4. Blind external peer review: reviewers evaluate originality, relevance, methodological rigor, evidence, clarity, and ethical compliance.
  5. Editorial decision: reviewers provide recommendations; the final decision remains an editorial responsibility.

IJLES does not guarantee a fixed review time.

Ethics Note

Authors are responsible for ensuring that their manuscript complies with IJLES research and publication ethics policies. Manuscripts involving human participants must include appropriate ethics approval and informed consent information.

AI tools may be used only to support language clarity or technical preparation. Any use of AI must be disclosed, and authors remain fully responsible for the manuscript's content, data, citations, and interpretation.
