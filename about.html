About IJLES

The International Journal of Language, Education and Society (IJLES) explores the intersections of language, education, and society. It welcomes original research, theoretical discussions, policy analyses, reviews, and scholarly essays addressing contemporary issues in linguistic development, educational practice, cultural interaction, and social transformation.

IJLES values interdisciplinary approaches and aims to support academic dialogue across diverse educational and social contexts, especially research that engages with underrepresented contexts and communities.

Journal Profile

Publication languageEnglish. Manuscripts must be submitted in clear and academically appropriate English.
Reference stylePublication Manual of the American Psychological Association, Seventh Edition (APA 7).
Publication frequencyBiannual, with two issues per year. Exact publication months will be announced when confirmed.
Submission methodOnline submission through the Submit Manuscript page.
Review modelInitial editorial screening followed by blind external peer review by three independent reviewers for suitable manuscripts.
Author feesIJLES currently charges no submission, processing, or publication fees.
Access modelOpen access. Published content will be freely accessible to readers without subscription charges.

Editorial Principles

  • Academic integrity and ethical publishing practice
  • Transparent editorial screening and decision making
  • Independent scholarly review
  • Interdisciplinary and international scope
  • Respectful, inclusive, and accessible academic communication
